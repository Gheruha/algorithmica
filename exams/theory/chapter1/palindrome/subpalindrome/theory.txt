Problem 56 — Longest palindromic substring

Algorithm A:
1. We iterate through all positions i from 1 to n.
2. For each position i, we consider two possible palindrome centers:
   - (i, i) for odd-length palindromes
   - (i, i + 1) for even-length palindromes
3. For each center, we expand to the left and right while the characters are equal.
4. When the expansion stops, the pointers have already gone one step outside the palindrome, so the palindrome length is r - l - 1.
5. We compute:
   - len1 = length of the palindrome centered at (i, i)
   - len2 = length of the palindrome centered at (i, i + 1)
6. We keep the maximum of all lengths found.
7. The algorithm returns the length of the longest palindromic substring.
